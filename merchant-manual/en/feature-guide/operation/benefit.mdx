title: Invite Rebates
---

This is a card-related marketing campaign. By leveraging referral relationships, it boosts user registration, card issuance, and spending activity, strengthening user growth and retention.

## 1. Campaign Settings
Used to create and maintain campaign rules. Merchants can flexibly configure invitation conditions, member levels, rebate rates, reward distribution, and clawback logic.

![Campaign Rules](/cn/images/huodonggz.png)

### Member Level Settings
Defines when a formal invitation relationship is established, and configures the rebate rate for each member level after the relationship is created.

**Valid Invitees**
An invite is considered valid based on the following conditions:
- Successful card issuance
- Successful KYC
- Successful registration

**Level reward settings**
You can set rewards for different tiers and upgrade based on the number of valid invitees.
- Direct invitation
- Indirect invitation

Rewards can also be triggered in different scenarios:
- After successful card issuance
- After successful spending settlement

<Note>
The system supports 2 tiers of invitation relationships:
- Direct invitation: A enters inviter code B during registration; A is B’s direct invitee.
- Indirect invitation: B enters code A during registration, and C enters B’s code; C is indirectly related to A.
</Note>

### Reward Settlement Rules
Defines the reward distribution mechanism to ensure risk control:

**Reward currency**
Select the currency used to pay rewards. Stablecoins (e.g., USDT) are recommended to reduce FX volatility risk.

**Settlement cycle**
Set the frequency of reward calculation and distribution.

**Reward claim threshold**
Set a minimum claimable amount to avoid frequent settlement of many small transactions and reduce operational costs.

**Reward clawback rules:**
If an invitee generates a refund, the inviter’s reward will be clawed back proportionally. By default, the amount is deducted from the inviter’s unclaimed reward pool and can go negative.

### Campaign Rule Copy
Manage the campaign period and operational copy so the frontend can clearly display the rules to users.

- Reward cycle
- Member level rules
- Reward introduction
- Reward distribution rules
- Reward claiming rules
- Reward clawback rules

## 2. Earnings Ranking
Shows members’ participation in the invite campaign, including invitation records and reward claiming/settlement/clawback records.

![Ranking](/cn/images/huodongph.png)

**Invitation performance:**
The member’s current campaign level, invitation reward stats, and invitees’ spending status.

**Invitation records:**
Records invited users, including activation status and time.

**Reward claim records:**
Records the member’s actual reward claims.

**Reward settlement records:**
Historical records of rebate settlements.

**Reward clawback records:**
If refunds occur, the system records clawback details in real time.

## 3. Invitation Records
View all invitation records and manually create invitation relationships.

- Inviter
- Invitee
- Invitation time

## 4. Commission Settlement
All settlement records for members participating in the invite campaign.

- Reward currency and amount
- Invitee ID and email
- Relationship (direct/indirect)
- Trigger action (spending settlement / successful card issuance)
- Rebate rate and settlement time

## 5. Commission Claims
Reward claim history for all members.

- Claimant ID
- Reward currency
- Claimed amount
- Payout time
